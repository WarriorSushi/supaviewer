# Research Notes

## What The Current Market Suggests

There is clear momentum around AI-native video as both a creation workflow and a consumer viewing format.

Three patterns stand out:

1. Creation platforms are becoming social.
2. Curation and festivals are becoming legitimacy layers.
3. Provenance and disclosure are becoming platform requirements, not optional nice-to-haves.

## Adjacent Players

### Generation Platforms Adding Social Surfaces

- OpenAI Sora has an explore/feed model alongside creation and sharing.
- Meta launched `Vibes`, a feed for discovering, creating, and remixing AI-generated videos.

Implication:

The biggest model companies are already moving toward social distribution. You should assume this gets more competitive, not less.

### Festival And Showcase Layer

- Runway's AI Film Festival has become a visible quality signal and partnered with IMAX for 2025 screenings.
- Multiple AI film festivals now exist globally.

Implication:

There is demand for prestige, curation, and "best of" surfaces. That supports a premium discovery strategy over a pure open-feed strategy.

### Community Layer

- Smaller communities and galleries exist, but the space is fragmented.
- There is still no universally trusted destination for AI-native video discovery across tools and styles.

Implication:

A cross-tool curation product still has room if it delivers taste, metadata, and credibility.

## Strategic Risk

If you build only an open feed, you will be competing against:

- YouTube distribution
- TikTok-style engagement loops
- model vendors with built-in feeds

That is a weak opening position.

If you build a curated discovery layer with creator identity, provenance, and strong taxonomy, the position is more defensible.

## Platform Constraint: YouTube First

A YouTube-first MVP is a good constraint, but it comes with product implications:

- playback depends on embed behavior
- creator ownership stays with YouTube
- your platform must add value beyond "another place to click play"

So your moat must come from:

- curation
- metadata
- creator discovery
- collections
- editorial packaging
- social proof inside your niche

## Compliance And Trust

You should treat disclosure and provenance as product pillars from the start.

Why:

- YouTube requires disclosure of realistic altered or synthetic content in relevant cases.
- major AI-video products are already using watermarking and provenance signals
- user trust will matter a lot in this category

Your product should support:

- creator self-declaration
- visible AI-made labeling
- provenance metadata storage
- moderation and reporting
- rights and consent policies

## Recommended Position

The most defensible early position is:

**A premium discovery and curation platform for AI-native cinema, starting with externally hosted content.**

This is stronger than:

- "AI TikTok"
- "AI YouTube clone"
- "just another gallery"

## Suggested Wedges

Choose one primary wedge first:

- AI-native cinema and cinematic storytelling
- AI music videos
- AI-native creator discovery
- curated best-of across models and tools

The strongest brand wedge is probably cinematic storytelling plus premium curation.

If you choose this wedge, the product should optimize for:

- watch sessions, not swipe sessions
- titles, collections, and creators, not endless clips
- immersive detail pages, not disposable cards only
